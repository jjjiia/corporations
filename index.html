This map visualizes the quantity, chronology, and geographical concentration of new foreign corporate entities in New York City throughout history. It is intended to show how the international businesses is situated in New York City's zip codes and how New York City based companies are distributed across the world.

We are interested in different forces that shape both the realities and the perceptions of neighborhoods in a city. The identity of neighborhoods, particularly in New York City, are greatly influenced by the mixture of different nationalities. This diversity is made partly visible at the street level by businesses catering to or offering services from particular countries.

The world heatmap showing the quantity of registrations confirm that english speaking countries such as Canada and United Kingdom are among the jurisdictions that have most companies registered in New York City. However there are also a few surprises.

This dataset dates back to 1883, allowing the historical aspects of the city to be reflected in the visualizations. Gabon, located on the west coast of central Africa, had a record number of companies registered in the city up to the mid 1900s. The timelines of Canada, United Kingdom, Japan, and Korea show a much less erratic profile than those of developing countries even when the quantities are comparable.
